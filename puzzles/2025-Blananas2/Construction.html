Construction

New mathematicians these days tapping away at the calculators on their phones...

  • Draw a circle centered at the origin with radius length 1.
  • Draw a circle centered likewise with radius length 7/9.
  • Draw lines parallel to the x = -y diagonal such that they intersect the y-axis at ±1/6.
  • Draw a square centered at the origin with side length 2.
  • Mark a point 0.5 below the x-axis on the square's left edge.
  • Mark a point 0.5 right of the y-axis on the square's top edge.
  • Draw a line connecting points.
  • Mark points where this line intersects either the x-axis or y-axis.
  • Draw lines perpendicular to the line which meet at these points.
  • Draw a line connecting where these lines intersect the square's right and bottom edges.
  • Rotate a copy of the previous figure by 180°.
  • Draw a horizontal line of length 2.
  • Draw a line starting at the midpoint of this line and ending 2 units above.
  • Draw lines starting at the endpoint of this line and ending at the ends of the other line.
  • Ignore the internal line of this figure for the remainder of the procedure.
  • Mark points at the center of all lines.
  • Draw lines connecting every pair of points.
  • Draw a circle centered at the origin with radius length √8.
  • Mark points on the circle intersecting the x = -y diagonal.
  • Draw lines tangent to the circle parallel to the x-axis.
  • Mark points distance 8 from the y-axis intersecting these lines in quadrants not occupied by points.
  • Draw lines tangent to the circle perpendicular to the lines.
  • Mark points distance 15 from the x-axis intersecting these lines in quadrants described above.
  • Label marked points from top to bottom A through F.
  • Draw connecting lines to create figure ACBFDEA.
  • Draw a square centered at the origin with side length 2.
  • Draw a circle arc centered 3/4 below the origin with radius length 3/4 starting at 270° going clockwise ending at the y-axis.
  • Draw an ellipse arc centered 11/10 below the origin with semi-major vertical axis length 27/20 and semi-minor horizontal axis length 81/80 starting at the point nearest the square's bottom left corner going clockwise ending at the y-axis.
  • Draw lines parallel to either the x-axis or y-axis distance ±3/4 from their respective axis.
  • Draw a line starting at the origin ending 3/4 to the right.
  • Draw a line starting where the ellipse arc intersects the y-axis ending infinitely right.
  • Mark a point 1/4 to the left of the top-rightmost intersection point of lines.
  • Draw a line connecting this point to the bottom-rightmost intersection point of lines.
  • Draw a line connecting the square's bottom-right corner to a intersection of a line and square nearest to the point.
  • Draw a circle centered 4/9 above the origin with radius length 9/20.
  • Draw circles centered 4/9 below the x-axis ±5/9 from the y-axis with likewise radii.
  • Draw lines starting at the center of the circle above the x-axis ending at centers of other circles.
  • Draw lines parallel to these lines, offset by ±0.13 along the x-axis.
