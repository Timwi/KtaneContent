On the Subject of 12trap

The Council of the Sparking Diamond... One fear. Twelve trap.

  • 12trap is a module that has twelve LED buttons that may be one of seven different colors, along with the symbol of the Council.
  • Pressing a button will select it and may also change the display. Pressing a different button will cause that button and the selected button to swap color, simultaneously deslecting the button. Pressing an already selected button will deselect it. Deselection will change the display to the symbol.
  • However, the LEDs will cycle clockwise on which button is “off”. There are two of these cycles. One cycle rotates at 2 RPM. Another rotates at 5 RPM. Selecting an “off” button will register a strike.
  • Set all twelve LED buttons to their correct color to defuse the module.

The Council of the Sparking Diamond is a terrorist organization - whose main goal is to create increasingly ever-more difficult situations involving explosives to cause chaos and destroy humanity.
They created fail safes on these explosives - “modules”, they call them - in case once we’re to go off in their warehouses or factories. If in the event one activates prematurely, employees may call into a trusted set of experts, because only they have the instructions to activate the fail safes in the form of manuals. The warehouses and factories go under a front company named Steel Crate Incorporated - a company that supposedly sells steel and other heavy-duty products.
In the beginning of their operations, it was found that bomb squads had figured out the properties of the modules on their own - police and select “daring unlikely heroes” we’re able to solve their 11 modules that they have painstakingly created. So within the next several years, it was an arms race to create ever more difficult modules to crack and beat these bombs squads. Soon, after harassing the powers of magnetic fields, black holes, levitation, acids, molecular structure, lasers, and many many more, over 2000 different module fail safes were in production. Each ever more complicated than before.
These modules were turn in by “Designers”, who wrote these manuals for each module to be modeled, coded, and distributed. And what could be thousands have been rejected, there were simply too many to know them all.
Every module’s original manual has been placed in an archive, a room named The Repository. A high-security heavily-maintained room which contains the written instructions to activate every failsafe still in production. However, after it was discovered, outsiders began attempting to reverse engineer the mechanisms of these modules as mere “challenges”. They knew they had to work faster.
So the organization got a few of these designers invited by a higher-up. 15 of them were to meet together for the chance to be permanently promoted into the Council of the Sparking Diamond. They were to be tested, to write manuals as quickly as possible in the span of only 48 hours to generate more and more modules. Whoever’s manuals was most worthy throughout the competition would be permanently given a seat.
Once three of them were eliminated after Round 2’s writing period was over, it was during the voting period that the Council decided to take twelve of the ideas that have already been generated from each member left and use them as part of an “entrance exam” to verify the identity of Council members. This project was codenamed “Twelve trap”. At the end of Round 3.... It’s here.

On the Subject of Beyond Re-Pairing

Breaking Rules / Keypad Directionality / Tie Breaker

  • This page is specifically for determining the colors of the three leftmost LED buttons.
  • Pressing any of the associated buttons will change the display to the same cycle of six arrows. These arrows are of different shapes, colors, and patterns.
  • These six arrows are always* able to be split into pairs based on one of the aforementioned properties of each arrow. By shape, by color, or by pattern.
  • Order the pairs by the first arrow that appeared in the cycle. There’s a blank slide after the last arrow before the cycle loops around.
  • Pairs are also ordered by which came first/second in the cycle.
  • Use the Order 2 Hexagonal Grid to perform the next step:
    For the first pair, start in the white cell. Follow the pair of arrow’s directions to land on a color, looping around to the opposite side of the grid if you run into a black border. The color that you land on is your first color of the sequence.
  • Follow the same instructions for the second and third pair to get a second and third color of the sequence respectively.
  • Input the color sequence into the module in order from bottom to top.

The primary colors of an RGB color model are Red, Green, and Blue
The secondary colors of an RGB color model are Yellow, Magenta, and Cyan
The absence of all three primary colors is Black.
The presence of all three primary colors is White.

A complementary color is the inverse of all primary color’s intensities.
