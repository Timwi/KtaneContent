On the Subject of Colored Squares

There is order in chaos. A pattern in the colors. Find it, and all will become clear.

See Appendix for identifying modules in Colored Squares family.

  • Press all squares in the correct group to progress the module.
  • Pressing a square will cause it to light up white. Make all squares white to disarm the module.
  • To begin, press the color group containing the fewest squares. If there is a tie, you are looking at a different module. Refer to the appendix below.
  • Then use the table to determine the next group to press in each stage.
  • “Group” refers to all squares of a particular color, or all non-white squares in the topmost row or leftmost column containing non-white squares.
  • Pressing an incorrect square will result in a strike and reset the module.
  • White squares will remain white for the duration of the module, but non-white squares may change color in each stage.

Appendix: Identifying brethren of Colored Squares

If there are 4 of the 5 colors (Red, Yellow, Blue, Green, and Magenta) occurring exactly four times each with exactly 1 color missing:
Bicolored Squares
If one color occurs fewer times than any other (no tie), and upon a successful press, the pressed button turns white:
Colored Squares
If three colors occur exactly twice each and two occur exactly 5 times each:
Decolored Squares
If four colors occur exactly once each:
Discolored Squares
If there are exactly 3 colors that occur only once:
Isocolored Squares
If there are colors besides Red, Blue, Green, Yellow, and Magenta, and there are no duplicate colors:
Juxtacolored Squares
If one color occurs fewer times than any other (no tie), and upon a successful press, the pressed button turns black:
Not Colored Squares
If there are 16 squares of a uniform color:
Overcolored Squares

Perspecticolored Squares
If there are colors besides Red, Blue, Green, Yellow, and Magenta, and there are duplicate colors:
Tombstone Maze

Uncolored Squares
If four colors occur exactly three times each:
Varicolored Squares
