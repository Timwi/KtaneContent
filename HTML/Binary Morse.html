On the Subject of Binary Morse

This module consists of two screens, a keypad, a submit button and clear button. The screen cycles an indefinite amount of decimal numbers per second.

These sequence of numbers, when converted into seven-digit binary numbers, show a repeated transmission of a morse code letter for each significant digit.

A dot is defined as one "1" between zeroes, while a dash is defined as three consecutive "1"s between zeroes. A single "0" between two "1"s is defined as a gap between two parts of a letter, while three consecutive "0"s is defined as a gap between whole letters.

Each significant digit would only transmit one letter at a time repeatedly. Determine the letters transmitted by each significant digit of the binary, then convert them into morse code.

From left to right, convert a dot to one "1", a dash to three consecutive "1"s, a space between two parts in a letter to one "0", and a space between two whole letters to three consecutive "0"s.

If the length of this binary string isn't divisible by 8, prepend zeroes until it is.

Next, split up the binary string into groups of 8, from left to right. Convert these groups into decimal numbers and take their sum.

Submit this number into the module to solve it. A strike will occur for a wrong answer, but will not reset the module.
