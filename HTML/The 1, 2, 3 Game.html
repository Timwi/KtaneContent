On the Subject of The 1, 2, 3 Game

Congratulations, you have been invited to The Genius Game.

  • A “The 1, 2, 3” module is a 3x3 grid of screens. The defining features of this screen arrangement are the top-right and middle-left screens contain an avatar and a nametag, with the middle-left screen reading “YOU”, and the bottom row of the grid of screens shows playing cards valued 1, 2, and 3 - with a counter representing how much you have left of each card displayed on the bottom left.
  • Play against your opponent in nine matches of The 1, 2, 3 Game to solve the module. If you win six of the matches, the module will solve. Otherwise, it will strike, and your submission will be reset.
  • You have three of each type of numbered card, for a total of nine. Your opponent also has the same set-up. Luckily, your opponent has predetermined what cards they will play and in which order. Even luckier, you’re The Genius. This means you are able to accurately predict which cards they’ll play and when and win as many matches as possible.
  • A match occurs when both players have placed down their cards and flip them over on the count of three. Only a player with the higher card will win and earn a point, seen on their counter. - In the case of a tie, neither player will earn a point.
  • Take the avatar and name of your opponent and look up the nine-card sequence both could potentially play. Compare both sequences against each other by imagining a hypothetical 1, 2, 3 Game between both card sequences.
  • The card sequence that has more wins against the other is the sequence your opponent is actually using. If in the case both sequences received the same amount of wins, the sequence that received all their wins faster is the sequence your opponent will actually use.

The 1, 2, 3 Game Module: Three Facts Summary

  1. Use the avatar and name of your opponent in the table below to determine what sequence they will play their cards in.
  2. Imagine both sequences playing against each other. The sequence with more wins is the sequence your opponent will play.
  3. Play nine matches against your opponent with the cards you have. Aim for 6 wins to receive zero strikes upon completion.
